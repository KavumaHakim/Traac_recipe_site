The Omelette

  1. Crack 2 eggs into a bowl and whisk.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan with butter over medium heat.
  3. Pour in eggs and cook until edges start to set.
  4. Add fillings (e.g. cheese, veggies, ham) to one half.
  5. Fold omelette in half and cook until fillings are heated through.

Beef stew

  1. Brown the beef cubes slowly in a large pot with some oil over high heat, until they're nicely browned on all sides.
  2. Add sliced onions, minced garlic, and chopped carrots to the pot; cook until the veggies are softened and fragrant.
  3. Pour in some rich beef broth, a spoonful of tomato paste, and a sprinkle of fresh herbs; stir everything together to combine.
  4. Let the stew simmer gently, covered, for 2-3 hours or until the beef is tender and falls apart easily.
  5. Season the stew with salt and pepper to taste, then serve it hot, garnished with fresh herbs, if desired.

Pasta

  1. To start, boil a large amount of salted water in a big pot, then add your preferred type of pasta, making sure to stir it gently to avoid tangling.
  2. Cook the pasta until it reaches the right consistency, which is usually when it still has a bit of firmness in the middle, after around 8-12 minutes.
  3. While you're waiting for the pasta to cook, heat some olive oil in a big frying pan over medium heat, then add your chosen ingredients for the sauce, such as minced garlic, chopped onions, and sliced tomatoes.
  4. AOnce the pasta is ready, drain the water and add the pasta to the frying pan with the sauce, mixing everything together until the pasta is evenly coated.
  5. Finally, serve the pasta hot, adding some grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves on top if you like.
